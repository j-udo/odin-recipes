Rice

Description

Jollof rice is a vibrant and flavorful West African dish made primarily from long-grain parboiled rice, tomatoes, onions, and a blend of spices. It is often cooked in a single pot, allowing the ingredients to meld together, creating a rich and deeply spiced base that infuses the rice with a reddish-orange hue. Typically, ingredients like bell peppers, garlic, ginger, and chili peppers are blended into the tomato sauce, giving the dish its signature taste—savory, slightly sweet, and a touch smoky when cooked properly. Depending on the region or family tradition, it may be prepared with vegetables, fried plantains, or proteins like chicken, beef, or fish.

Ingredients

  • Long-grain parboiled rice – 2 cups
  • Tomatoes (fresh or canned) – 4 medium or 1 cup canned
  • Red bell peppers – 2 large
  • Onions – 2 medium (1 for the sauce, 1 sliced for cooking)
  • Garlic cloves – 2–3
  • Fresh ginger – 1 tablespoon, grated
  • Tomato paste – 2 tablespoons
  • Vegetable or chicken stock – 2–3 cups
  • Vegetable oil – 1/4 cup
  • Bay leaves – 1–2
  • Salt – to taste
  • Black pepper – to taste
  • Thyme – 1 teaspoon
  • Curry powder – 1 teaspoon
  • Paprika or chili powder (optional, for heat) – to taste

Steps

  1. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, 1 onion, garlic, and ginger into a smooth mixture.
  2. Rinse the rice thoroughly. You can parboil it briefly to reduce starch, then drain.
  3. Heat oil in a large pot. Sauté sliced onions, then add tomato paste and fry for a few minutes. Add the blended mixture and cook until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate (about 15–20 minutes).
  4. Add thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, salt, black pepper, and paprika/chili (if using). Pour in the stock and bring to a simmer.
  5. Stir in the rice, making sure it’s evenly coated. Cover the pot with foil or parchment paper under the lid to trap steam.
  6. Let it cook on low heat for 25–35 minutes, stirring occasionally and adding small amounts of water or stock if needed to prevent burning.
  7. Once the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed, fluff with a fork. Optionally, stir in cooked meat or vegetables. Serve hot.
