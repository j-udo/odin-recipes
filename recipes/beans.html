Beans

Description

Nigerian beans and plantain bowl is a hearty, comforting dish that combines soft-cooked stewed beans with sweet, caramelized fried plantains. Often made with black-eyed peas or honey beans (ewa oloyin), the beans are simmered in a rich tomato-onion sauce seasoned with traditional spices. The result is a creamy, savory base that pairs perfectly with golden, crispy slices of ripe plantain.

Ingredients

  • Honey beans (or black-eyed peas) – 2 cups
  • Ripe plantains – 2
  • Tomatoes – 3 medium
  • Onion – 1 large
  • Garlic – 2 cloves
  • Palm oil or vegetable oil – 1/4 cup
  • Salt – to taste
  • Seasoning cubes or powder – to taste
  • Crushed chili pepper or Scotch bonnet (optional, for heat)
  • Water – as needed for boiling

Steps

  1. Rinse and boil the beans in water until soft (about 45–60 minutes). Drain excess water if needed.
  2. Blend the tomatoes, half the onion, and garlic into a smooth paste.
  3. In a separate pot, heat palm or vegetable oil, sauté chopped onions, then add the blended sauce and cook until thickened and fragrant.
  4. Season the sauce with salt, seasoning cubes, and chili pepper (if using).
  5. Add the cooked beans to the sauce and simmer for another 10–15 minutes, allowing flavors to combine.
  6. Peel and slice ripe plantains, then fry in hot oil until golden brown on both sides.
  7. Serve the beans hot with the fried plantains on the side or on top.
